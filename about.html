Hi! I am Srimantee Mohanta.

Student // Artist // Photographer // Musician


Welcome to my Website! I am currently studying in 12th grade at Garden High School, one of the leading English-medium coeducational schools in Kolkata. I love taking pictures of the little intricate beauties of everyday life and people. With art and posters as well, I aim to be able to capture essence in its rawest form. However, I equally enjoy observing and absorbing art, in literature, film and music. I truly believe in the cause for taking a stand against those posing threat to human rights, human life, obviously, the protection of our environment. I aspire to bring definitive social change through helping others who may lack a platform to voice their struggles freely.

Significant Project Experiences

‘Exploring Water Quality in Eastern India and Kentucky’ by University of Kentucky, US Consulate & ASED India
International-Level School Representative (June 2017 - June 2018)
This research project conducted over the span of a year consisted of water quality testing, analysis and presenting our findings and suggesting viable solutions to the growing issues faced in the East Kolkata Wetlands.
‘English through Literature: Shakespeare’ by British Council, Kolkata
Summer School Participant (May 2017 - June 2017)
This experience introduced me to the variety in Shakespearian literature- in writing style, how plots unravelled, and their cultural impact, by holding interactive discussions on sections from the different genres.
‘SHANTA: Voyage musical en INDE’ by Tout S’Metisse and LaCaZa Musique
Vocal Artist (September 2018)
In this project, I worked with a French-Indian team creating a collaborative children’s book called “Shanta: A Musical Voyage to India”. I was one of the vocal artists featured on the exclusive CD that accompanied the book.
‘GHSiMUN: United Nations Security Council (2018) and United Nations Human Rights Council (2020)’ by Garden High School
Country Representative for Syria (July 2018) and Country Representative for Germany (October 2020)
In my second and third experience participating in an Model United Nations Committee, I had a chance to familiarize myself with the official international standards of formalities and decorum. Along with preparing and delivering speeches in the concerned agendas, I aided allies in passing a Resolution and also faced rebuttal questions from fellow delegates and the Chief Executive Board.
‘India International Guitar Festival’ by Bhattacharya School of Universal Music
